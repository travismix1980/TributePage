Sir Terry Pratchett

A timeline of a great writer

  • 1948 - Terry Pratchett was born in Beaconsfield, Buckinghamsire

  • 1962 - At 13 years old, Terry Pratchett published his first story, Business Rivals in his school magazine The Technical Cygnet

  • 1963 - The story Business Rivals was published commercially as The Hades Business, and then bought his first typewriter from the proceeds

  • 1965 - Terry Pratchett leaves school to take up a job at a local newspaper where he wrote a weekly childrens column. Some of these stories have been published in Dragons at Crumbling Castle

  • 1968 - While interviewing Peter Bander van Duren, a local publisher he mentioned a book that he was working on which lead to Terry meeting, Colin Smythe who became Terry's first publisher and later his agent. The book was The Carpet People Terry's first novel. He also married his wife, Lyn

  • 1971 - The Carpet People was published, with the launch party held in the carpet department of Heal's on Tottenham Court Road. Terry was only 23 years old

  • 1976 - Rhianna Pratchett, Terry's daughter was born

  • 1979 - Terry moves out of journalism to become a press officer for four different nuclear power stations. Terry blames this on his 'unerring sense of timing'

  • 1983 - Terry's first Discworld novel, The Colour of Magic was published by Colin Smythe, starting a phenomenally successful series consisting of 41 books

  • 1986 - The third Discworld novel, Equal Rites which covered emerging topics such as feminism was broadcast on the radio. It was the most popular book the station had ever broadcast

  • 1987 - With the Discworld series now having four books, and becoming more and more successful, Terry was finally able to quit working for the Electricity Board and become a full time writer.

  • 1996 - The Times reported that Terry Pratchett was the bestselling author in the UK

  • 1998 - Terry was appointed OBE in the Queen's Birthday Honours List. Despite initially suspecting it was an elaborate hoax, he showed up to accept the award.

  • 2002 - Terry won the Carnegie Medal for his children's book The Amazing Maurice and His Educated Rodents Even though he ended up winning many awards and even knighthood, this was his favorite

  • 2007 - Terry was diagnosed with a rare form of Alzheimer's, Posterior Cortical Atrophy. After deciding to tell the world, he began to campaign for the awareness of the disease

  • 2008 - Terry dontated a million dollars to Alzheimers research

  • 2009 - Terry was knighted by the Queen for services to literature. 'My greatest service to literature was to avoid writing any' - Terry Pratchett

  • 2010 - Terry delivered the annual Dimbleby Lecture on BBC1, with the help of his friend Tony Robinson. Tony read Terry's speech for him entitled Shaking Hands With Death. This was the most watched Dimbleby Lecture

  • 2011 - Terry presented a documentary on assisted dying. Terry Pratchett: Choosing to Die, in which he travelled to Switzerland to witnes the death of a terminally ill motor neurone sufferer Peter Smedley at the Dignitas clinic. The documentary won both a BAFTA and an Emmy.

  • 2013 - Despite the progress of his PCA, Terry, still busy, published his 40th Discworld novel, Raising Steam, as well as The Long War with Stephen Baxter, The Science of Discworld IV with Ian Stewart and Jack Cohen, and Dodger's London

  • 2015 - Terry died peacefully at home in Wiltshire, with his family around him and his pet cat asleep on his bed. He had finished one final Discworld novel, The Shepherd's Crown, a few months earlier

'It's not worth doing something unless you were doing something that someone, somewere, would much rather you weren't doing.' - Terry Pratchett
